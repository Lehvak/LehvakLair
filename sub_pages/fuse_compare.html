Fuse Compare

There are 3 main types of fuse sets defined by fuseline 01:

  • Retail
    Fuseset 01: 0F0F0F0F0F0FF0F0
  • Test
    Fuseset 01: 0F0F0F0F0F0FF00F
  • Dev
    Fuseset 01: 0F0F0F0F0F0F0F0F

As we can tell from the above fuseline 01, in very simplistic terms, the last two bits signify what content the machine will be
0F being Dev signed
F0 being Retail signed
The two bits before will tell us if the system will have kernel debugging locked down.
F0 = Locked down
0F = Unlocked
With that being said, we can determine that a Test kit is much closer to a consumer retail unit than to a dev kit. This explains why internally Microsoft's SKU tracking of some XDK group units, certain units are referred to as 'Retail' when they are running Test fuses.
